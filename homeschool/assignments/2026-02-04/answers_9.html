üìú History ‚Äî Mormon Migration

1. The Mormons had to leave because angry mobs attacked their town and their leader Joseph Smith was killed in 1844.
2. They began their journey on February 4, 1846.
3. They traveled over 1,000 miles, and the trip took more than one year.
4. Any two of: on foot (walking), by horse, by covered wagon.
5. Accept any two well-reasoned answers, such as: They had to cross mountains and rivers / They traveled in winter when it was freezing cold / They had limited food and supplies / The distance was very long (over 1,000 miles) / They were being chased by angry mobs.

üåä Science ‚Äî The Water Cycle

1. Evaporation: Water from lakes, rivers, and oceans heats up from the sun and turns into water vapor (gas) that rises into the air. It is caused by heat from the sun.
2. Condensation: As water vapor rises high into the sky, it cools down and turns back into tiny water droplets. These droplets gather together to form clouds.
3. Precipitation: When clouds get heavy with water, the water falls back down to the ground. Two examples: rain and snow. (Also acceptable: sleet, hail.)
4. Accept any valid reason, such as: It brings fresh water to plants, animals, and people / Without it, there would be no rain and crops couldn't grow / It keeps water moving so the Earth stays healthy.
