Thursday, February 5, 2026

Word of the Day

Resilient: Able to withstand or recover quickly from difficult conditions.

This Day in History

1907: Leo Baekeland announces the creation of Bakelite, the first synthetic plastic.

Scripture of the Day

"And lo a voice from heaven, saying, This is my beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased."

— Matthew 3:17

Toddler (Age 2)

Reading & Writing: Point to the letter 'B' for Baptism. Finger paint circles in blue paint to look like water bubbles.
Math: Count 3 blue blocks (to represent water).
Science: Water Play! Use a bowl of water and see which toys float and which ones sink.
Faith Thought: Look at a picture of Jesus being baptized. Say "Jesus is God's Son." Jesus loves me!

Primary (Age 6)

Math: Practice your "plus-threes"! Solve: 5+3, 7+3, 2+3, and 10+3.
Reading & Writing: Read a short story about John the Baptist. Practice writing: "I can be kind like Jesus."
Science: Static Electricity: Rub a balloon on your hair and see if you can pick up small pieces of paper!
History: What is a King? Talk about how Kings used to rule countries (linked to King Leopold II in history).
Faith Thought: Draw a picture of a dove. The Holy Ghost came like a dove when Jesus was baptized. The Holy Ghost can help us feel peace.

Elementary (Age 9)

Math: Multiplication Review: Complete a 10x10 multiplication grid. Focus on the 7s and 8s.
Reading & Writing: Read Chapter 5 of your current book. Write a paragraph about a time you felt the Spirit or did something hard.
Science: Learn about "Bakelite" and the invention of plastics. Why was it such a big deal for the world?
History: The Mexican Constitution: Research why the 1917 constitution was important for the people of Mexico.
Faith Thought: Jesus was perfect, but he still got baptized to "fulfill all righteousness." Why is it important for us to follow His example?

Junior High (Age 13)

Math: Algebra: Solve for x in these equations: 3x + 5 = 20; 2(x - 4) = 12.
Reading & Writing: Read an article about Gamel Abdel Nasser and the United Arab Republic. Write a 3-sentence summary of the Pan-Arabism movement.
Science: Chemistry: What is a polymer? Research how synthetic plastics like Bakelite are formed at a molecular level.
History: The formation of the United Arab Republic (1958). Why did Egypt and Syria try to become one country?
Faith Thought: Compare the accounts of Jesus' baptism in Matthew 3, Mark 1, and Luke 3. What unique details does each author include?
