Village Homeschool Daily Plan

Thursday, February 5, 2026

Word of the Day: Diligence – Steady, earnest, and energetic effort.

This Day in History: In 1971, Apollo 14 astronauts Alan Shepard and Edgar Mitchell landed on the Moon!

Scripture of the Day: "And as all have not faith, seek ye diligently and teach one another words of wisdom; yea, seek ye out of the best books words of wisdom; seek learning, even by study and also by faith."
— Doctrine and Covenants 88:118

Preschool (Age 2)

Math: Count 5 stars with Mom or Dad. Can you point to each one?
History/Science: Moon Wonders. Look at a picture of the moon. It's high in the sky! Say "Moon."
Reading: Picture Book Time. Read a story about animals or the stars.
Writing: Salt Tray. Practice drawing "O" shapes (like the moon) in a tray of salt or flour.
Faith Thought: Jesus loves to see you learn! Say: "I am a child of God, and I am learning."

Elementary (Age 6)

Math: Addition Mission. Solve: 5 + 5, 10 + 4, and 2 + 8.
History: Apollo 14. Alan Shepard was the first American in space. He even hit a golf ball on the moon!
Science: Moon Phases. Draw what the moon looks like tonight. Is it a circle or a banana shape (crescent)?
Reading: Read for 10 minutes from your favorite book.
Writing: Write 3 sentences about what you would do if you visited the Moon.
Faith Thought: Diligence means trying our best. When we work hard on our schoolwork, we are using the talents Heavenly Father gave us.

Intermediate (Age 9)

Math: Multiplication Mastery. Practice your 7s and 8s tables for 15 minutes.
History: The Space Race. Research why the United States wanted to land on the moon. What was the "Apollo" program?
Science: Gravity & Orbits. Why doesn't the moon fall down to Earth? Explain how gravity and speed keep the moon in orbit.
Reading: Read one chapter of your current novel.
Writing: Write a paragraph about the Apollo 14 landing. Use the word diligence in your description.
Faith Thought: D&C 88:118 tells us to seek from the "best books." Choose one book you are reading and share one good thing it has taught you.

Junior High (Age 13)

Math: Linear Equations. Solve for x: 3x + 5 = 20 and 2(x - 4) = 10.
History: Cold War Competition. Analyze how the Apollo 14 mission served as a symbol of technological superiority during the Cold War era.
Science: Lunar Geology. Research "Maria" (the dark spots on the moon). What are they made of, and how did they form?
Reading: Read 20 pages of your current literature selection.
Writing: Persuasive Writing. Should the government spend more money on space exploration (returning to the Moon/Mars) or on solving problems on Earth? Write a 3-paragraph argument.
Faith Thought: Discuss: How does seeking learning "by study and also by faith" change the way you approach difficult subjects like Math or Science?
