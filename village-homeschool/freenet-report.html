Freenet Research

Exploring Decentralized Use Cases for 2026

Executive Summary

The modern Freenet (formerly Locutus) is a peer-to-peer platform for cryptographically secure, scalable, and verifiable applications. Unlike blockchain, it handles high-frequency state updates efficiently using WebAssembly (WASM) contracts. It is "Infrastructure-less," meaning the network is the database.

Proposed Application Concepts

1. The Village Registry

Decentralized Registrar: A way for parents to sign academic credentials that are stored permanently and verifiably on Freenet.

Value: Records stay valid even if the Village servers disappear. They are cryptographically immune to tampering.

2. Open Marketplace

P2P Lesson Trading: A global marketplace for lesson plans without a central platform taking a percentage of the sale.

Value: Absolute censorship resistance. No central authority can remove content or block transactions.

3. Ghost Supply Chain

Local Food Clusters: Coordinating inventory (produce, milk, supplies) within a neighborhood without relying on the public internet.

Value: Resilience during infrastructure failures. Data remains available as long as local peers are connected.

4. Living Encyclopedia

High-Scale Collaborative Wiki: A real-time editing platform that can merge millions of edits simultaneously without a central host.

Value: Combines the scale of Wikipedia with the security of a private, decentralized network.
