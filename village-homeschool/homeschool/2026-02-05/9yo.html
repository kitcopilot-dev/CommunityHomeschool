Daily Inspiration

Word of the Day: Luminary - a person who inspires or influences others, especially one prominent in a particular sphere.

This Day in History: In 1919, United Artists was founded. It allowed actors like Charlie Chaplin to control their own work and profits, changing Hollywood forever.

Faith-Based Learning

Scripture of the Day: James 1:5 - "If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God, that giveth to all men liberally, and upbraideth not; and it shall be given him."

Faith Thought & Activity: Wisdom isn't just knowing facts; it's knowing how to use those facts to help others and make good choices. Activity: Read the story of Joseph Smith's First Vision (Joseph Smith—History 1:11-13). How did reading this scripture change his life? Write one way you can seek wisdom in your own life today.

Academic Content

Math

Multiplication & Division:

  • Multiply: 45 x 7 = ?
  • Divide: 124 ÷ 4 = ?
  • Word Problem: If you have 3 boxes of 12 pencils each, and you give away 8 pencils, how many do you have left?

History

The California Gold Rush: Research the year 1849. Who were the "49ers"? Write down three items a miner would need to take with them to find gold.

Science

The Water Cycle: Draw and label a diagram showing Evaporation, Condensation, and Precipitation. Explain why the sun is important for the water cycle to work.

Reading

Comprehension: Read a chapter from your current book. Write a 5-sentence summary of the main events. Who was the most important character in this chapter?

Writing

Creative Writing: Imagine you found a secret door in your house that leads to a library that has every book ever written. What is the first book you look for and why? (Write at least 2 paragraphs).
